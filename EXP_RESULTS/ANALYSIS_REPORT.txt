  Part 3. Key Finding 1: Taleb Override Dominance Phenomenon
  (The Override Dominance Phenomenon)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[Phenomenon]

  All non-Taleb expert scores in C1/C3 are extremely compressed to the 0.02–0.08 range.
  Only Taleb maintains high scores of 0.72–0.98.

  Causal chain:
    1. WDSS scenario tail risk indicators trigger the Taleb formula
    2. Taleb normalized_score = 0.72–0.98 (almost always HIGH)
    3. Phase 1 Soft-Shutdown: influence_factor = (1 - taleb_score)^decay_power
       → (0.15)^2.8 ≈ 0.004
    4. Other 11 experts' scores = original score × 0.004 → ~0.03

[Verification: no_taleb ablation]

  Score changes upon Taleb removal:
    Graham:   0.029 → 0.540 (18.6× increase)
    Buffett:  0.038 → 0.632 (16.6× increase)
    Shannon:  0.050 → 0.851 (17.0× increase)
    Ensemble: 0.033 → 0.754 (22.8× increase)

[Implications]

  This is not a bug but intended system design behavior.
  The phenomenon where a single risk expert dominates the entire 12-expert system
  is an extreme empirical demonstration of "predictable causality (TAF)."

  From a paper perspective, this reveals the duality of structured ontologies:
  - Advantage: The risk expert's judgment propagates deterministically and predictably
  - Disadvantage: Excessive concentration eliminates other experts' perspectives

  Part 4. Key Finding 2: OFS Reversal Phenomenon
  (The Faithfulness Paradox)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[Expected vs. Actual]

  Expected: C1 OFS >> C2 OFS (formulas should be more faithful than text)
  Actual: C2 OFS (0.818) >> C1 OFS (0.632) >> C1_fair OFS (0.458)

[Root Cause Analysis]

  Ground Truth is based on a "simplified ontology interpretation":
    "If the indicator exceeds the threshold → HIGH, otherwise → LOW"

  However, the actual engine pipeline is more complex:
    raw_score = weighted_sum(metric_checks)
    step3 = raw_score × win_rate × decay_factor
    normalized = sigmoid(step3)
    phase1 = normalized × override_factor (Taleb)

  This multi-stage multiplication causes scores to deviate from the original "intent."

[Paper Framing]

  "The Faithfulness Paradox of Exact Implementation"

  Exactly executing formulas (exact implementation) and
  faithfully following expert intent (intent faithfulness) are
  not the same thing.

  The formula engine exactly executes multi-stage corrections
  (win_rate, decay, regime, override), but these corrections
  distort the original expert's simple intent of
  "if this indicator is good → HIGH."

  LLMs intuitively follow the "intent" expressed in text,
  resulting in higher intent faithfulness.

[Revised Paper Narrative]

  Title: "The Faithfulness Paradox: When Exact Implementation
         Diverges from Expert Intent in Multi-Agent Systems"

  Core Thesis:
  "Exactly executing formulas (computational faithfulness) and
   faithfully following expert intent (intent faithfulness) are
   fundamentally different dimensions.

   The structured ontology engine:
   (a) Exhibits predictable causal behavior (TAF 80%), but
   (b) Its multi-stage pipeline distorts original intent (OFS 0.458), and
   (c) Strong override mechanisms eliminate diversity (CDI 0.167)

   The LLM persona engine:
   (a) Shows high intent faithfulness (OFS 0.818), but
   (b) Lacks causal interpretability, and
   (c) Has unpredictable internal logic even at temperature=0

   This trade-off opens the question not of 'which approach is better?'
   but 'in what context does which type of faithfulness matter?'"

  Part 10. Ground Truth Limitations and Remediation
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[Current Ground Truth Limitations]

  Ground Truth v2.0 is based on "simple threshold interpretation":
    "If the indicator exceeds the threshold → this expert will give a HIGH score"

  However, the actual engine goes through a more complex pipeline:
    Step 3: × win_rate (0.54–0.99) × decay_factor (0.83–1.0)
    Phase 1: × override_factor (~0.004 when Taleb fires)
    Phase 2: Global Adjustment (macro weight)
    Phase 3: Synergy/Suppress

  Result: Even when Ground Truth predicts "the formula should give HIGH,"
  the actual pipeline gives LOW due to multiplicative corrections.

[Remediation Options (Future Work)]

  Option A: Pipeline-Derived Ground Truth
    → Re-derive Ground Truth from the actual engine pipeline output (without override)
    → C1_fair OFS = 1.0 (by definition), making this meaningless

  Option B: Adaptive Threshold
    → Use the median of C1 score distribution as threshold
    → Measure relative OFS per distribution

  Option C: Maintain current approach but reframe interpretation
    → Define "Ground Truth = simplified representation of expert intent"
    → Interpret OFS as "intent faithfulness" (not formula accuracy)
    → This is the most honest and interesting framing

  ★ Recommendation: Option C → Transparently describe Ground Truth limitations
     in the paper and position "The Faithfulness Paradox" as the core contribution

    → This is the core value of structured systems.

[Key Finding 1: Phase Transition Phenomenon]

  OFS drops sharply from 0.9236 → 0.6319 between k=0.5 → k=1.0.
  This resembles a "phase transition":

    k < 1.0: Override is moderate, preserving the HIGH/LOW direction of expert scores
             → OFS 0.92 (intent faithfulness preserved)
    k ≥ 1.0: Override is strong enough to compress scores below the threshold
             → OFS 0.63 (intent distortion begins)

  Paper significance: In structured systems, a single parameter (k)
  determines the boundary between "intent preservation" and "intent distortion."
  This empirically demonstrates the interpretive importance of hyperparameters
  in neuro-symbolic systems.

[Key Finding 2: OFS Plateau Phenomenon]

  OFS is perfectly constant at 0.6319 for k ≥ 1.0.
  Reason: As k increases, score magnitude continues to compress,
  but the HIGH/LOW classification (threshold 0.5) is already determined at k=1.0.
  Larger k values only push already-below-threshold scores even lower.

[Key Finding 3: Monotonic Decrease of CDI]

  CDI systematically decreases with increasing k:
    k=0.5: CDI=0.631 (healthy differentiation)
    k=3.5: CDI=0.167 (extreme homogenization)
    k=4.0: CDI=0.135 (minimum)

  Exception: CDI rebounds slightly to 0.208 at k=5.0.
  Cause: Under extreme compression (mean=0.014), subtle differences
  between experts (0.001–0.03) become proportionally large.

[Key Finding 4: Optimal k Value Derivation]

  At k=0.5, OFS=0.92 and CDI=0.63, all metrics are optimal.
  The current default k=3.5 has excessive override strength.

  However, rather than claiming k=0.5 is "optimal" in the paper:
  "The system's faithfulness-diversity tradeoff changes decisively
  as a function of k" is a more appropriate interpretive finding.

[Paper Utilization Strategy]

  This result is highly suitable for Figure visualization:
    X-axis: k value, Y-axis: OFS/CDI (dual axis)
    → The phase transition in the k=0.5–1.0 range is visually dramatic

  Key message: "In a structured system, a single causal parameter controls
  the entire system's faithfulness and diversity.
  Such interpretability is impossible in LLM-based systems."

  Part 15. Supplementary Experiment 1: Adaptive Threshold OFS
  (Adaptive Threshold OFS — Resolving the Paradox)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[Experimental Design]

  Compute the median from C1_fair expert score distribution and use it as an adaptive threshold.
  Compare with fixed OFS (threshold=0.5) to analyze threshold dependency.
  No LLM calls (re-analysis of existing results).

[C1_fair Score Distribution]

  n=288, median=0.6333, mean=0.6868, std=0.1794
  min=0.3486, max=0.9802

  Interpretation: C1_fair experts produce scores averaging 0.69.
  The 0.5 threshold is "too low" for C1_fair, so nearly all scores are
  classified as HIGH → mismatch with ground truth LOW predictions → OFS declines.

[Key Finding: Reversal of The Faithfulness Paradox!]

  Fixed threshold 0.5: C2 (0.818) >> C1_fair (0.458) — C2 wins
  Adaptive threshold 0.633: C3_fair (0.879) > C1_fair (0.823) > C2 (0.776) — C1/C3 win!

  The paradox is **reversed**:
  - When the threshold is calibrated to C1_fair's score distribution,
    the structured engine achieves higher OFS than LLM
  - C3_fair (no edges) achieves the highest OFS = 0.879
  - This means that even with pure formulas alone (without edge interactions),
    high intent faithfulness can be achieved

  This strengthens the paper's message:

  → But "faithfulness is context-dependent, and this dependency itself is the finding"

[TAF Direct Comparison: C1 (80%) vs C2 (33%)]

  This can serve as the paper's key weapon:
  The structured system (C1) successfully predicted 4 out of 5,
  while the LLM system (C2) succeeded in only 1 out of 3.

[Failure Analysis: Why Do Predictions Fail in C2?]

  1. c2_no_taleb failure:
     In C1, Taleb removal → Phase 1 override released → all scores rise.
     This is an effect caused by "system interaction."

     In C2, Taleb is merely an independent LLM call.
     Removing Taleb has no effect on the other 11 experts' scores.
     The ensemble decreased because in 9 scenarios, Taleb's score > other experts' mean.
     Removing a high-scoring contributor reduces the ensemble.

     Key point: In C1, the effect of Taleb removal is "liberation through interaction,"
     while in C2, it is "simple contributor removal." This difference is the root cause of prediction failure.

  2. c2_no_risk failure:
     In C1, removing all RSK → override released → dramatic score variance increase.
     In C2, removing 3 experts → 12→9 experts → sum of remaining 9.

     Score variance decreased because:
     The risk experts (Taleb, Shannon, Thorp) frequently assign
     extreme scores (0.0 or 1.0) per scenario → contributing to variance.
     Removing them leaves only the remaining experts' more moderate scores,
     actually reducing variance.

  3. c2_no_graham success:
     This is the simplest prediction: Graham is the core contributor
     to the Value domain, so removal decreases A3_TOXIC's Value score.
     This prediction depends only on direct contribution without interaction effects,
     so it holds in C2 as well.

[Deep Insight: The Presence of Interaction Effects Determines Predictability]

  Common trait of successful C1 TAF predictions:
    → All based on "system interactions" (override, synergy, suppress)

  Common trait of failed C2 TAF predictions:
    → Applied C1's interaction effects to C2,
      but such interactions do not exist in C2

  The only C2 TAF success:
    → Based on "direct contribution" rather than interaction

  Conclusion: The core value of structured ontologies is
  explicitly modeling "expert interactions" to make
  their effects predictable a priori.
  In LLM systems, experts execute independently,
  making interaction-based predictions inherently impossible.

[Paper Utilization Strategy]

  Present as a direct comparison table:
    "C1 TAF: 80% (4/5) vs C2 TAF: 33% (1/3)"

  Key message:
    "The structured system can predict the direction of component removal
    with 80% accuracy. The LLM system achieves 33%, which is lower than
    random guessing (50%). This empirically demonstrates the unique
    causal interpretability of structured systems."

  Note: While C2 TAF 33% being below 50% is intriguing,
  statistical significance cannot be claimed with n=3.
  In the paper, describe as a "directional tendency" while
  contrasting with C1's dramatic causal effects (22.8× increase).
