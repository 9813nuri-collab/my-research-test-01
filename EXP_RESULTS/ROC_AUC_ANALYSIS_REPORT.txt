1. MOTIVATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The original analysis reported a "Faithfulness Paradox": the formula-engine
conditions (C1, C3) achieved lower Ontology Faithfulness Scores (OFS = 0.46)
than the LLM persona condition (C2, OFS = 0.82) under a fixed threshold
tau = 0.5, despite executing expert rules exactly.

Two competing hypotheses were proposed:

  (H1) THRESHOLD ARTIFACT: The OFS gap is an artifact of tau = 0.5 being
       poorly calibrated for the score distribution produced by the override
       mechanism. An adaptive threshold would close the gap.

  (H2) STRUCTURAL DISCRIMINATION FAILURE: The formula engine genuinely
       produces less discriminative scores (HIGH-expected vs LOW-expected
       experts are harder to separate), regardless of threshold.

This supplementary analysis resolves which hypothesis is correct by computing
threshold-INDEPENDENT evaluation metrics (ROC-AUC), where no threshold
choice can bias the result.

2.3 Feature Construction
  For each condition, for each (expert, scenario) pair:
    - Continuous score: expert_scores[expert_id] averaged across repetitions
    - Binary label:     1 (HIGH) / 0 (LOW) from ground truth

  Total data points per condition: 12 experts x 24 scenarios = 288

2.4 Metrics
  - ROC-AUC:  sklearn.metrics.roc_curve + auc
    Interpretation: probability that a randomly chosen HIGH-expected
    (expert, scenario) pair scores higher than a LOW-expected pair.
    Completely threshold-independent.

  - OFS-Threshold Sweep:  OFS(tau) = accuracy at threshold tau
    Computed over tau in [0.01, 0.99] (300 steps) to visualize the
    Faithfulness Paradox reversal and locate the crossover point.

4. KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FINDING 1: The Faithfulness Paradox Is Partly a Threshold Artifact
-------------------------------------------------------------------
At tau = 0.5, C1 OFS = 0.46. At tau = 0.633, C1 OFS = 0.82 — matching C2.
This confirms H1: a significant portion of the OFS gap disappears with
an adaptive threshold calibrated to the override-shifted score distribution.
The OFS-threshold sweep (fig_ofs_threshold_sweep.png) visualizes this
reversal clearly.

FINDING 2: The Override Mechanism Has a STRUCTURAL Discrimination Cost
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Even threshold-independently, C1 AUC (0.746) < C2 AUC (0.898).
This confirms H2 is also true: the Taleb Master Override does not merely
shift the score distribution — it actively collapses HIGH/LOW score
separation by driving ALL expert scores toward zero simultaneously when
tail risk is detected.

When Taleb fires (A3_TOXIC, A4_HYPE scenarios):
  - HIGH-expected experts: score ~ exp(-Taleb * 3) -> near 0
  - LOW-expected experts:  score ~ exp(-Taleb * 3) -> near 0
  Both groups converge, making them indistinguishable by any threshold.
  This produces the characteristic "flat middle segment" visible in C1/C3
  ROC curves (TPR stalls at ~0.57 across FPR = 0.05 to 0.45).

FINDING 3: Without Override, Structured Systems Dominate LLMs
-------------------------------------------------------------
C1_fair AUC = 0.971 >> C2 AUC = 0.898
C3_fair AUC = 0.939 >> C2 AUC = 0.898

Removing the override reveals that the formula engine, when evaluated
at the individual expert level, is substantially more faithful to
ground truth than LLM personas — in a threshold-independent sense.
The Faithfulness Paradox (C1 appearing less faithful than C2) was
ENTIRELY driven by the override mechanism.

FINDING 4: Graph Structure Provides Modest Incremental Benefit
--------------------------------------------------------------
C1_fair (Full Ontology Graph) AUC = 0.971
C3_fair (Flat, No Graph)      AUC = 0.939

Delta = +0.031 from graph interaction edges.
The graph structure (Taleb->ALL, Graham->Buffett, etc.) provides
meaningful but modest improvement in individual expert faithfulness
when operating without override suppression. The dominant factor is
override presence/absence (+0.225 AUC), not graph depth (+0.031).

5. THEORETICAL ANALYSIS: THE TWO-LEVEL FAITHFULNESS PROBLEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This analysis reveals that "faithfulness" must be evaluated at two
distinct levels that can produce opposing conclusions:

LEVEL 1 — Individual Expert Formula Faithfulness (OFS / ROC-AUC)
  Question: Does expert X's output score match expert X's intended
            formula output, evaluated independently?
  Result:   C1_fair >> C2 >> C1 (override collapses this)

LEVEL 2 — System-Level Design Intent Faithfulness
  Question: Does the system behave as the designer intended when
            considering cross-expert interaction rules?
  Result:   C1 executes override PERFECTLY — this is the design.
            C2 cannot be evaluated on this dimension (no explicit rules).

The core insight: the Taleb Master Override IS the design intent at
the system level, but it APPEARS as a faithfulness failure at the
individual expert level because ground truth is defined for experts
in isolation (without accounting for interaction suppression effects).

This mismatch constitutes what we call a MEASUREMENT LEVEL MISMATCH:
  - Metric defined at: individual expert scope
  - Mechanism operating at: multi-agent interaction scope
  - Result: valid metric, wrong scope -> misleading conclusion

7. IMPLICATIONS FOR THE FAITHFULNESS PARADOX NARRATIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Original paper narrative (before this analysis):
  "C1 OFS = 0.46 < C2 OFS = 0.82 at tau=0.5, but this reverses with
   adaptive tau = 0.633. The paradox is a threshold artifact."

Refined narrative (after this analysis):
  "The Faithfulness Paradox has a two-layer structure:
   Layer 1 (Threshold Artifact): OFS at tau=0.5 underestimates formula-
   engine faithfulness because override shifts the score distribution.
   Adaptive tau closes this gap (confirmed by OFS-tau sweep).
   Layer 2 (Structural): Even threshold-independently (ROC-AUC), C1
   scores lower than C2 (0.746 vs 0.898). This is not a measurement
   artifact but an emergent property of the override mechanism:
   Taleb suppression collapses HIGH/LOW score separation as a
   deliberate risk-control feature. C1_fair (override removed) achieves
   AUC = 0.971, exceeding C2, demonstrating that the formula engine
   itself is more faithful than LLM personas — the override mechanism
   alone creates the apparent deficit.
   Therefore, the Paradox is resolved: structured systems appear less
   faithful because they are MORE faithfully executing their own
   system-level design, which includes intentional individual-score
   suppression under tail risk conditions."
